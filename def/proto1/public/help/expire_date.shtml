Expire Date

Enter the prescription's expiration date. This is the date when the prescription runs out and does not allow refills.

For short term medications, such as antibiotics for strep throat, the expiration date is not important, as prescriptions for most maintenance medications will expire in one year. For controlled substances, like codeine or valium, the prescription normally expires in six months or less. The expiration date will be on the prescription bottle and will be called the Expiration Date. Enter this date for long term medications so you can see which medications will need a new prescription prior to the expiration date.
