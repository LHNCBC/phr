What Can the Personal Health Record Do for Me?

The Personal Health Record (PHR)

  • Organizes key health information about you and your medical dependents.
  • Prints reports that you can deliver to your doctor or other appropriate medical professionals.
  • Exports electronic copies of your Personal Health Records to your computer.
  • Provides a means of updating medication history, laboratory test results, changes in medical conditions, and other health information.
  • Provides one-click access to reliable consumer health information about relevant medical conditions, drugs, allergies, and vaccines through the use of the PHR's Info Page links.
  • Reminds you about medical tests, treatments, vaccines, and health behaviors that might be beneficial to your health or the health of your dependents. Reminders are based on the information you store in your record (or dependent's record) and on published scientific recommendations. Note: These reminders are only guidelines for discussion with your doctor and should not be considered a medical diagnosis.
  • Allows you to keep a record of questions to ask your doctor.
